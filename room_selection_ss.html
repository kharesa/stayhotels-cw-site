Spenser Stopover

Open spaced dining area
Multiple conference areas
Moments away from green park station
Gym facilities on the 1st & 2nd floors

The Spenser Stopover is the closest you'll get to a sleepover with the Queen without a state visit invitation. The hotel is directly across from the royal residence on property that enjoys a colorful past – the Duke of Buckingham lived here in the 1600s, followed by an apothecary and a Court dressmaker. The Rubens, which was built in 1912, housed the Polish army during WWII and gave respite to exiled Charles de Gaulle. The hotel's decadent – and often equally colorful – rooms and suites are a comfortable home base in the epicenter of London.

The newly built Hotel is equipped with smart hotel rooms systems and newest trends and technologies. Elegant, modern, with personalised and impeccable service offering comfortable accommodation and top-quality gastronomy. This site is a place of business success and pleasant company
